Department of Telecommunications, Government of India

DoT notifies TCS Rules amendments, institutes Mobile Number Validation and IMEI scrubbing to curb telecom-enabled fraud

•

The Department of Telecommunications notified Telecommunication Cyber Security (TCS) Amendment Rules, 2025 on October 22, 2025, and affirmed they are fully in force and enforceable.

•

A Mobile Number Validation (MNV) platform is institutionalised to let service providers verify that a mobile number genuinely belongs to the account holder, reducing mule accounts and identity fraud.

•

The rules mandate ‘Resale Device Scrubbing’, requiring sellers to check every second‑hand device IMEI against a centralised blacklist to curb stolen, cloned, or blacklisted handset circulation.

•

The amendment creates a new regulatory category, Telecom Identifier User Entities (TIUEs), covering banks, fintechs, e‑commerce and others that use telecom identifiers for authentication or services.

•

TIUEs will have regulated obligations to share specific telecom‑identifier data with the government under defined circumstances, balancing investigative coordination with data protection norms.

•

DoT clarified an inadvertent republication on October 29, 2025 was rescinded via a November 25 notification, and that the original October 22 amendments remain valid and enforceable.

Amit Agrawal

Haryana to roll out investor-friendly City Gas Distribution policy, says Industries & Commerce Secretary Amit K Agrawal

•

Haryana is preparing a modern, investor-friendly City Gas Distribution (CGD) policy to accelerate natural gas pipeline and distribution infrastructure across the state.

•

The state will incorporate Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB) suggestions to align the CGD policy with national standards and industry expectations.

•

Under the Draft CGD Policy 2025, authorised companies can seek Right of Use (RoU) and Right of Way (RoW) permissions via the state’s single-window portal.

•

Applicants must submit a Common Application Form with GIS-based route maps, work plans, land details, and chosen technology specifications such as HDD or trenchless boring.

•

A transparent fee structure and simplified financial procedures are introduced to streamline approvals and reduce administrative friction for investors and developers.

•

Commissioner and Secretary Amit K Agrawal said the policy updates the 2010 framework to reflect current technological and administrative requirements for CGD development.

•

Officials expect the policy to boost investment, expand cleaner natural gas use in industry and households, and strengthen Haryana’s energy security and ease of doing business.
